Making Smart Homes Accessible to Every Indian

We started Blinkdrop because we believed home automation shouldn't cost a fortune or require an electrician's degree — it should just work.

Our Story

Blinkdrop was born out of a simple frustration: existing smart home systems were either too expensive, too complex, or simply not designed with Indian homes in mind. Our founder, an engineer who'd retrofitted his own apartment with open-source smart switches, realized he could do this for everyone.

In 2024, we set out to build a smart home ecosystem that fits the reality of Indian electrical infrastructure — existing switchboards, variable power supply, diverse home layouts — without demanding any rewiring or professional setup. The result is Blinkdrop.

Today, we serve thousands of homes across India, from compact Mumbai apartments to sprawling Bengaluru villas. Our devices connect seamlessly with Google Home, operate locally over MQTT without needing the cloud, and are engineered with the kind of security that protects your home data the way a physical lock protects your front door.

Our Mission

Our Technology

Under the hood, Blinkdrop runs on a stack built for reliability and speed. Our backend API is built with Node.js and Express, backed by MongoDB, and deployed on Render. The frontend dashboard is hosted on Vercel for global CDN performance. Devices communicate in real time via the MQTT protocol — the same standard used in industrial IoT systems worldwide.

Google Home integration is powered by the Google Smart Home Actions framework, with state reports sent asynchronously to minimize latency. Our JWT-based authentication includes token versioning, enabling instant remote logout across all devices — a feature rarely seen in consumer home automation products.
